“Symphonie fantastique”

The Romantic period in music started in the early 1830s and continued until the turn of the century. It was characterized by the experimentation of form and the focus of the individual. During this time music became more expressive and orchestras generally increased dramatically in size. Symphonie Fantastique describes the journey of an artist and relates to the influence of globalization in the romantic era. It conveys how the development of a globalizing culture led to more modern methodologies to engage in escapist behavior.

“Submerged”

This relates to the initial development of modern music and its relation to the movements in the 1960s and 1970s. During the 20th century, political tensions and globalizations drove the development of escapist behaviors in the creation and experience of music.

“Ensemble"

Musicians traditionally wear black. During the renaissance era music was intended for the wealthy while musicians were predominantly middle class. They wore black in order to blend into their surroundings. This piece conveys the duality that exists for musicians in which they are both seen and unseen. They are humanized and dehumanized, relating to the abstraction of perspective in the experience between the artist and the viewer.

“Allegro”

This piece relates to the idea of division in the contemporary world and how music develops a sense of connection between individuals, escaping the barriers introduced by modern technology and allowing us to live in the temporary illusion of unity.

“Bebop”

Jazz breaks the structural and expressive rules of traditional music, allowing for improvisation and spontaneity. However, the modern jazz world maintains many elements of elitism conveying how commercialization of human expression affects our ability to escape the limits of society.

“Vivace”

During the pandemic, the idea of escapism took on a prominent hold over the public mindset as the confines of our homes provided physical barriers that framed our reality. This changed the experience of consuming media. This piece centers around the experience of live music during the pandemic and the surrealism of the social change that occurred. It develops the coexistence of livelihood and bleakness that characterized the attitudes towards connection during the pandemic.

“Accelerando”

This piece focuses on the elitism that dominates the art world through the idea of “orchestra chairs”. Music and the arts are often difficult to access by different groups of people but we tend to perceive them as universal, which ultimately limits our capability to access new music. Through diversifying musical interests we are able to limit the way elitism and privilege drive many areas of the art world

“Arpeggio”

This piece describes how art allows us to connect to a time when we were less aware of the issues within this world and less capable of understanding the circumstances of our own life. It describes my experiences in how my perspectives and approaches towards art and musical expression were altered through both maturity and the escapism that comes from the preservation of childhood innocence.

“Andante”

This describes how we compartmentalize different areas of our lives and how separate art and the people behind pieces.
